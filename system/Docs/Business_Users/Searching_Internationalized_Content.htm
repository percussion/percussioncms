Searching Internationalized Content

When you perform searches on content created in different languages, you can search on a string in the language of one of your Locales to locate only items created in that language. For example, if you search Content Items that may be in German or English on the text string "small," all or most of your results will be Content Items in English, but if you search on the string "klein," all or most of your results will be Content Items in German.

However, searches work differently if you search on keywords. Keywords are predefined choices for filling a Content Item field. For example, the choices for filling a Department field could be:

  • Human Resources
  • Education
  • Accounting

Each of these keywords has an identification number that is also used for versions of the same keywords in other languages. Therefore, the keywords might appear as:

  • Personal Wesen
  • Ausbilding
  • Rechnungs Wesen

in the German Locale, but:

  • Personal Wesen and Human Resources
  • Ausbilding and Education
  • Rechnungs Wesen and Accounting

would have the same identification numbers.

If you create a custom search that looks for items with a certain Department keyword, Rhythmyx searches on the keyword's identification number and returns Content Items that use the keyword in any of its translations. For example, if you search on the keyword "Human Resources" Rhythmyx would return English Content Items that use "Human Resources" and German Content Items that use "Personal Wesen."
