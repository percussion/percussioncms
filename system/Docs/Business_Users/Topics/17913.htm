Boosting a Term

When you boost a word or phrase in a search term, you indicate that a document matching the boosted word or phrase is a more significant match than a document that matches another word or phrase in the term. To boost a term use the caret, "^", symbol with a boost factor (a number) at the end of the term. The higher the boost factor, the more relevant the term will be.

Boosting allows you to control the relevance of a match. For example, if you are searching for

jakarta apache

and you want the term "jakarta" to be more relevant, boost it using the ^ symbol along with the boost factor next to the term. In Search for, enter:

jakarta^4 apache

This makes documents with the term jakarta more relevant than those with the term apache. You can also boost phrase terms as in the example:

"jakarta apache"^4 "Apache Lucene"

By default, the boost factor is 1. Although the boost factor must be positive, it can be less than 1 (e.g. 0.2)
