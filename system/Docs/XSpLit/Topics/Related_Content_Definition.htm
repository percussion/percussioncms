Related Content Definition

You should generally use the <div> tag to define related content.  If the Slot contains no HTML or XML mark up, or if it is in a table (where the <div> tag is not allowed) you can use the <span> tag.  

A related content definition uses the the slotname attribute to define the name of the Slot.

<span slotname="Related Features">Slot example</span>

When split, this code would produce the following XSL:

<xsl:variable name="rxslot-1">

<rxslot template="RelatedFeatures">

<xsl:copy-of select="$related/linkurl[@slotname='Related Features']/>

</rxslot>

</xsl:variable>

<xsl:apply-templates select="$rxslot-1" mode="rxslot"/>

Rhythmyx generates a unique nodeset variable name for each slot it encounters.  The nodeset variable name takes the format rxslot-n, where n indicates the numeric order of the slot in the template, in this example, rxslot-1.  The following code shows an example:

<rxslot template="RelatedFeatures">

   <linkurl slotname="Related Features" slotid="42" variantid="23">

      <Value current="http:. . . . . . . . .1.html"/>

   </linkurl>

   <linkurl slotname="Related Features" slotid="42" variantid="12">

      <Value current="http:. . . . . . . . .2.html"/>

   </linkurl>

   <linkurl slotname="Related Features" slotid="42" variantid="23">

      <Value current="http:. . . . . . . . .3.html"/>

   </linkurl>

</rxslot>
