Coronavirus Updates

Campus Communication Concerning Services for Modified Spring Semester 2020 Schedule

Updated on March 13, 2020

Posted at2:30 p.m.
Information for residential students revised on March 16 at 4:30 p.m.

Personal attention is a hallmark of the BW experience and you can expect that attention to continue, even if we are not on the same campus together. Your understanding and continued cooperation during this challenging time is greatly appreciated.

Baldwin Wallace University has implemented a Modified Spring Semester Schedule in response to COVID-19, commonly known as novel Coronavirus.

There are currently no campus-associated cases of COVID-19.

However, to help reduce the risk of transmission, we have ceased face-to-face classes and will begin remote instruction on March 23, 2020. Our goal is to decrease the number of instances that require our community members to gather in groups. During this Modified Spring Semester Schedule, overall operations of the University will continue. Faculty may continue to use their offices and labs. All campus services will remain accessible to serve students.

BW is taking the following actions:

  • March 16-20: BW is extending Spring Break for all students to allow faculty and staff to finalize preparation for remote instruction.
  • March 23 - April 10: Classes will be held via remote instruction.
  • April 13: Face-to-face classes will resume.

Modified Spring Semester Schedule Details:

  1. Baldwin Wallace University remains open and all offices and student services are operating.
  2. All residential students are required to return to their homes during the Modified Spring Semester Schedule by Friday March 20, 2020 - April 10, 2020. Under special circumstances (international students, student employees who are designated as essential by their supervisor, or special hardship cases), students will be permitted to remain in on-campus housing during this timeframe. Students who must remain on campus are required to complete an Extreme Circumstances Housing Intent form at bw.erezlife.com by Tuesday, March 17 at noon. Contact (440) 826-2114, reslife@bw.edu. Students who need to return to residence halls to gather possessions necessary to attend remote instruction must do so by Friday, March 20 at noon. All swipe access will be turned off on March 20 at noon to students not remaining on campus during the remote instruction period. All students will again have access to their rooms when the remote instruction period ends. View more details for residential students >
  3. Remote instruction via the Blackboard platform will begin on Monday, March 23 and continue through Friday April 10, 2020. Students will be contacted by their professors through Blackboard messaging to begin instruction. Information regarding changes in specialized instruction such as laboratory, art or musical instruction will be provided before remote classes begin on March 23. Face-to-face classes will resume on Monday, April 13, 2020. April 10 remains a BW holiday in observance of Good Friday. Monday, April 13 is now a full instruction day. April 30 previously Ovation Day of Excellence is now a day of instruction. Ovation Day of Excellence is canceled.
  4. IT HelpDesk is ready to assist students and faculty during the remote instruction period. Go tohelp.bw.edu for support resources.
  5. All University sponsored events are canceled through April 26.
    1. Varsity athletic events: BW is following NCAA and Ohio Athletic Conference (OAC) direction and has cancelled all current championship competitions and 2020 spring sports seasons, effective March 16 through the remainder of the academic year. This includes all athletically related activities as defined by NCAA.
    2. Admission events: All large group admission events are canceled through April 26. Small group information sessions with admission counselors and individual and family campus tour sessions may still be scheduled. Graduate interviews and small group info sessions will continue as scheduled. Contact Admission Office at (440) 826-2091 or visit@bw.edu.
  6. Effective March 12, 2020, Baldwin Wallace is immediately canceling participation in all study abroad programs. All students studying abroad are required to return home. All 2020 May-term faculty-led programs are canceled. View more details on spring 2020 Study Abroad program cancellations and May-term 2020 faculty-led Study Abroad program cancellations.
  7. Campus Cleaning Service (Aramark). Continues to provide comprehensive campus cleaning with additional focus on high touch point areas.
  8. Dining Services will operate on a modified basis. View dining updates >
  9. Health Services is open. All students needing assistance should call before coming to the Health Center so that the staff can give them appropriate direction. View health services updates >
  10. Counseling Services will shift to industry-approved telehealth counseling appointments starting Tuesday, March 17. All currently scheduled appointments for Monday, March 16 are canceled in order to develop policy/procedure protocol for the change to telehealth. View counseling services updates >
  11. Career Services is offering phone or video conferencing to accommodate the remote instruction period. Individual in-person appointments are available. View career services updates >
  12. Lou Higgins Recreation Center will be open on March 16 after thorough cleaning and disinfecting. Hours will be Monday-Friday 6 a.m. - 6 p.m., Saturday 8 a.m. 2 p.m., closed Sundays, through April 9.
  13. Community Arts School classes and ensembles are temporarily suspended until April 13, 2020. Private lessons may continue as normal at the discretion of the instructor and student. View Community Arts School updates >
  14. Off-campus interns and LHE students: LHE students are expected to return to campus on March 16 to continue campus internship assignments. Students in internship or clinical rotational assignments off-campus should maintain their work schedule unless their host site alters their employee policy.
  15. Work study, student employees and on-campus interns are able to work under the approval of their supervisor.
  16. All University sponsored commercial carrier travel for faculty or staff is suspended through April 13, 2020. Faculty or staff who are returning from professional or personal travel abroad or cruise ship travel are expected to follow after-travel CDC guidelines.

We will continue to monitor information regarding the virus and will align our actions with the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), the World Health Organization and other city, state and federal agencies. Changes to the plans outlined above or additional information will be shared on this webpage as soon it is made available.

Prevention

It's worth noting that we are in the midst of cold and flu season and the presence of respiratory symptoms, including fever, cough and shortness of breath, does not mean the source of illness is coronavirus. As is the case during every cold and flu season, we are all encouraged to follow the CDC's best practices for preventing the transmission of respiratory illnesses:

  • Wash your hands often with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. If soap and water are not available, use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.
  • Avoid touching your eyes, nose and mouth with unwashed hands.
  • Avoid close contact with people who are sick.
  • Stay home when you are sick.
  • Cover your cough or sneeze with a tissue, then throw the tissue in the trash.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces.
Further Guidance and Information

Specific to coronavirus/Covid-19, guidance from the CDC suggests those with flu-like symptoms who have traveled from China within the last 14 days (or been in contact with someone believed to have contracted this strain) should seek medical attention and contact the provider before going.

For more information about novel coronavirus, the CDC,Ohio Department of Health (ODH) and Cuyahoga County Board of Health (CCBH) are trusted sources of information.

You may also reach out to BW Health Services at (440) 826-2178.
